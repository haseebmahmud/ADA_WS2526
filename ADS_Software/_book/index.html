Short course description

Using practically relevant examples the students practice how to use a statistic software to perform statistical analysis and test models.

In this course the students

  1. postulate a research question using their own data or a panel data set provided by the lecturer,

  2. postulate hypotheses to test,

  3. perform statistical analysis to test their models,

  4. learn to use software for their data analysis during computer lab sessions,

  5. write a scientific presentation and

  6. present their results in a plenary session.

Intended learning outcomes (ILOs) for Programme and Course

ILOs for the program ILOs for the course
Demonstrate profound knowledge in major fields of international business After the course the students are able to formulate a research question, derive hypotheses based on literature, identify an appropriate research design, specify and operationalize constructs, analyze data according to the hypotheses, and interpret the results. They are familiar with and can apply various models and methods of data assessment. The students know about probability of error, and the difference between significance and effect size when assessing results.
Implement conceptual knowledge and apply problem solving ability in different business contexts

The students learn how to test conceptual frameworks with empirical data. They will know how to decide for the appropriate statistical procedure depending on the research problem and characteristics of the data.

The students are capable of applying the imparted knowledge to draw the correct conclusions when it comes to making decisions in business environments based on data.
Apply digital skills to effectively resolve work-related assignments

The students are capable of conducting a well-founded literature research using databases and scientific search engines.

They learn to use statistical software (e.g, SPSS, Python, R, Stata) in a targeted and appropriate manner to analyze data for various research questions.
Work effectively in intercultural teams and leverage diversity The students will work in teams to conduct a research project. They need to discuss the approach and find a common strategy to manage the project timely and successfully. To achieve this, they must define milestones, allocate work packages, adhere to agreements, and reflect on their different working styles.
Behave in an ethical and sustainable manner The students learn about ethical conduct in scientific research, with special emphasis on plagiarism, data manipulation, and the presentation and interpretation of results.

Course outline

Theoretical part

  1. What is research, formulating and clarifying the research topic
  2. Literature review
  3. Theoretical framework and hypothesis development
  4. Questionnaire design
  5. Sampling design
  6. Measurement: scaling, reliability and validity

Applied part

  1. Basic statistical concepts: descriptive statistics (numerical and graphical)
  2. Data management
  3. Hypothesis testing
  4. Categorical data analysis, comparing two means
  5. Correlation and covariance
  6. ANOVA
  7. Reliability analysis
  8. Linear analysis
  9. Logistic regression

Exam form and evaluation

The final grade will be a combination of the result from a research project and a classroom examination.

  • Research project (50% of the final grade): Theoretical and empirical model including theory, research question/hypotheses, detailed derivation of hypotheses from the literature, methodology, descriptive results, model variables and regression analysis, scientific discussion, literature (minimum requirement: seven scientific references (journal articles), APA citation style), plus submission of dataset and syntax (final evaluations and model) + output regression analysis. Format: Presentation on the last lecture date with PowerPoint presentation and paper (max. 8 pages without appendix)

  • Individual test (50% of the final grade): Date and time will be announced later
